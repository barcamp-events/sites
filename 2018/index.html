About

Sat, Aug 4, 2018
KANEKO | 8 AM - 4 PM

A WORK IN PROCESS

At BarCamp, we've ditched the predetermined speaker list. We're a user-generated, creative unconference that provides a space for everyday humans to share their passions. Talks aren’t scheduled until just before the conference starts, and anyone can speak — though it's just as rewarding to sit back and listen. There's no bar (except for drinks at the opening party) and no camp (unless you bring your own tent). But there's a whole lot to discover.

This year, we celebrate process. How do we get from A to B, and where do our creative worlds collide? BarCamp 2018 invites you to share your messy, extraordinary, perhaps unfinished creations — because we're all a work in process.

> Hi-Fi House

Get pumped for the opening party, brought to you by Adobe! Each BarCamp ticket purchase gets you one free drink ticket. FAQs

What does my BarCamp ticket include?

  • Opening party and drink ticket
  • Grab-n-go breakfast and individual lunch from Panera
  • Coffee, soda and snacks
  • BarCamp 2018 t-shirt sponsored by Agape Red
  • Instruction manual and goodie bag
  • The chance to speak about your process, projects and passions
  • The chance to hear your favorite creatives speak
  • Opportunities to make new, possibly lifelong friends
  • An immersive experience in one of Omaha’s most curious, creative events

How much is it to attend?

  • $30 non-AIGA member
  • $25 AIGA member
  • $20 student (non-AIGA member)
  • $15 student (AIGA member)

Are there scholarship opportunities?

Yes! Thanks to Emspace + Lovgren, we’re able to provide 10 scholarships to our BarCamp community. Apply by July 14, and we’ll notify scholarship recipients by July 17. [link to scholarship form here]

What’s the ticket cost breakdown?

Thanks to our generous partners, a portion of BarCamp costs are already covered! Here’s where your ticket costs go.

  • 20%: event venue
  • 20%: opening party venue and drink
  • 20%: breakfast, lunch and snacks
  • 20%: t-shirt
  • 20%: ongoing AIGA Nebraska programming

Do I have to give a talk?

Absolutely not! Many BarCampers just want to meet new people and listen to talks. We encourage you to participate however you’re most comfortable. That’s the beauty of the unconference — you can pretty much do whatever you want, and it’ll be rewarding.

If I do give a talk, will I have any help?

This year, we’re providing each track with two speaker aides from FireBelly Rep, who will help calm your nerves and troubleshoot your tech. Tech Omaha will provide a video recording of your talk and an extra special BarCamp 2018 souvenir.

How long are the talks?

Each talk runs for 23 minutes. Speakers are allowed to use that time however they wish — you can even share a time slot with multiple people or set up a panel discussion, allowing time for Q&A at the end. The last 7 minutes of every half-hour slot is reserved for transition time between talks.

Can I give a sales presentation?

Nope! To be respectful of our BarCamp community, sales presentations or pitches are not allowed. Don’t even try it, folks.

What should I bring?

Feel free to bring your notebook, a stylus and your business cards. (We’ll also provide you with a BarCamp instruction manual where you’ll have ample room to take notes and exchange contact info.) If you’ve planned a talk with presentation slides, bring your laptop or an external drive with your files — we encourage you to use Google Slides to make everything a little easier. And maybe bring a clean pair of underwear, because you know the creative process gets messy.

When do I get my BarCamp t-shirt?

Tees are sponsored by Agape Red and will be available on the day of BarCamp at the merch table. Though we try to purchase enough of each t-shirt size, we recommend picking your shirt up early in the morning just in case.

Is my ticket transferrable?

If you’re unable to attend BarCamp, please contact Molly Misek at barcampchair@nebraska.aiga.org to transfer or refund your ticket. Ability to do so expires after July 31, 2018.

What if I have a food allergy?

No sweat! We make sure everyone is included. If you have specific food allergies or restrictions, email barcampchair@nebraska.aiga.org and we’ll take care of you. Each year we work with our caterer to provide vegetarian, vegan and gluten free options.

What is AIGA Nebraska?

AIGA Nebraska advances design as a professional craft and connects creative fields of work to generate action, provide inspiration, and foster diversity & inclusion in our community. To learn more and become a member, visit our website. Check out our code of conduct.
